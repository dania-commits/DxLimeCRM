## üß© What the demo contains

The project is a single-page interactive web app with six sections:

### 1. Customer Lens ‚Äì Personas & Jobs-to-be-done

A small module with three personas:

- Sales Manager at a growing SMB  
- Account Executive with a busy pipeline  
- Founder / CEO of an early-stage company  

Each persona shows:

- Jobs to be done  
- Pain points today  
- Where Lime Go can help  

This mirrors the ‚Äúdiscovery‚Äù side of the role and keeps me anchored in the user rather than just the feature.

---

### 2. Discovery Notes ‚Üí Product Problem

A free text area where I can paste or write discovery notes from calls and meetings.

The app then generates a **clear, outcome-focused product problem** to work on:

> sales teams waste energy on the wrong leads and struggle to understand which opportunities are most likely to convert.

The logic is intentionally simple but demonstrates how I like to quickly move from *noise ‚Üí narrative*.

---

### 3. Funnel Impact Simulator

This part is about **business impact**.

You can adjust:

- Monthly new leads  
- Baseline win rate  
- Improved win rate with better prioritisation  
- Average deal value  

The app then calculates:

- Baseline monthly revenue  
- Monthly revenue with improved focus  
- Extra revenue per month  

and plots a small comparison chart.

This shows how even a modest win rate improvement can be meaningful at scale, and connects product decisions to numbers.

---

### 4. Outcome-first Framing

A small set of bullet points that describes what ‚Äúsuccess‚Äù should look like if we ship the right prioritisation feature in Lime Go.

This is how I like to talk about features internally:
- from outcomes first  
- then behaviours  
- then specific UX ideas.

---

### 5. Initiative Prioritisation

A simple table with a few example initiatives, each with:

- user value  
- business value  
- effort  

A lightweight score is calculated and can be used to sort the table.

The idea here is not to enforce a specific framework, but to show that I think in **systems and trade-offs**, not feature wishlists.

---

### 6. Thin-slice Roadmap

An outline of how I would roll this type of capability out in phases:

1. Simple insight layer inside Lime Go  
2. Experimentation and A/B testing  
3. Playbooks and next best actions  
4. Regional tuning and advanced analytics  

This reflects the ‚Äúscaleup reality‚Äù from your ad ‚Äì shipping slices of value, learning, and iterating, not waiting for perfection.
